# About

*Episcopal Priest, Educator*

## Introduction

I was ordained an Episcopal priest in 1982, serving parishes in Indiana, Wisconsin, and Hawaii. In 2012, after 30 plus years in  pastoral ministry, I retired from active ministry, and since then I have continued to supply on a part-time basis in various parishes in South Carolina, North Carolina, and Florida.

I joined the Winthrop faculty in 2014 as an adjunct in the Department of Philosophy & Religion. From 2011-2014 I was on the faculty at Chaminade University in Honolulu as both and Instructor and an Assistant Professor in the College of Education. I did graduate study in Theology, Liturgy, and Hebrew at Notre Dame University. After my degree I taught for three years at St. Joseph's College in Rensselaer, Indiana. 

After moving to Hawaii and during my tenure as a rector I taught Bible studies at Iolani School during the summers. A research area of particular interest of mine is the pedagogy of religious education. At Chaminade University I taught and directed a graduate level course on "Peace, Social Justice and Educational Reform". 

My courses at Winthrop included: 

  * Introduction to the Hebrew Bible, 

  * Reading Biblical Texts, 

  * Introduction to Religious Studies, 

  * Modern Christian Thought
