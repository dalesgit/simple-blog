# Embarasement and Shame

Quiet day of prayer. 9 o’clock in the morning the Miata didn’t turn over but then on about the third try there was a pop. One more try and it started. Temperature was 75 but presumably it had been rising all morning long so the temperature is clearly a factor.

The point of the blogs for me was to find my voice. To counteract my sense of marching to somebody else’s drums. Feeling like I was working for somebody else.

It started with the question related to embarrassment and shame. I wondered what is it about my embarrassment and shame? I’m not talking about the kinds of things that caused embarrassment and shame when I was 16. Then it was things having to do with sexuality, relationships, drug use – things having to do with psychedelics and manufactured spiritual experiences. Easier than to talk about my experiences on the ski slopes then it was the orange pill that I took before shopping for my first car with my father.

But by my 20s and I felt myself drawn into the world of Christian spirituality and practice the subject of my embarrassment changed. Already before I had graduated. I had made the decision that still vividly rings today within me. I knew that intense spiritual experiences, mystical life, was possible in Christianity because I had seen evidence of it, and while my peers were making choices, headed for cults, existentialism, classicism, I decided I would follow the path of Christianity as far as it was fruitful. 

That led me ultimately to ordination and a path that included graduate school and the hodgepodge life I’ve lived since. But until the present I had never felt that same shame and embarrassment as when I admitted my Christian proclivities to my friends in my 20s.

What was it then? Was I selling out? To the establishment? To the oppressor?

What is the relationship between embarrassment and being ashamed? I know that shame is bound up with both of those feelings. And I have some sense of the way in which shame interacts with guilt and combines one’s sense of unworthiness. is that similar to the embarrassment I feel at belonging to a Christian church that wants nothing to do with the world around me, a world that has fallen into deep injustice and cruelty? A world where the perpetrators claim a Christian pedigree?
